Technical Architecture

BRAID deploys analytical workflows as containerised Task Execution Service (TES) requests within the three-layer TREvolution architecture:

Submission Layer

Manages the submission of analytics tasks from researchers to the federated infrastructure. BRAID workflows are packaged as containers and submitted via the GA4GH TES API.

Controller Layer

Governs disclosure-control validation and workflow orchestration. Ensures outputs — spatial maps, summary statistics, trained models — pass disclosure checks before egress.

Execution Layer

Handles secure execution of computational workloads within each TRE node (DPUK, NUH), maintaining provenance, auditability, and regulatory compliance throughout.

Standards & Frameworks

Five Safes Framework

All TRE operations follow the Five Safes: safe people, safe projects, safe settings, safe data, and safe outputs.

FAIR Data Principles

All datasets and workflows are Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, and Reusable. BIDS format is used for all neuroimaging data.

ISO 27001 / Cyber Essentials

DPUK operates under ISO-27001 certification. University of Nottingham holds Cyber Essentials Plus certification.

UK GDPR & Data Protection Act 2018

All activities comply with UK General Data Protection Regulation and the Data Protection Act 2018.
