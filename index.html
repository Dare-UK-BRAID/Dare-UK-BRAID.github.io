BRAID
Brain Frailty Integrated
through Data Federation

A multi-institutional project testing federated Trusted Research Environments to advance privacy-preserving, data-driven brain health and frailty research across the UK.

Explore the Research DARE UK Project Page ↗

Project Overview

Our brains, like the rest of our bodies, can be affected by age and disease — impairing brain health. Poor brain health may not lead to immediate clinical symptoms, but it predicts worse outcomes across many diseases. Researchers use the term brain frailty to describe cumulative brain damage that raises the risk of mortality, slower thinking, memory problems, and conditions such as dementia or stroke.

To study brain health effectively, researchers need access to large, diverse populations with data including brain MRI scans, clinical records, genetic data, demographics and lifestyle factors. Because this data is sensitive, researchers use Trusted Research Environments (TREs) — secure digital spaces allowing approved experts to analyse data safely without moving it.

The Challenge

Federated analysis — running analyses across multiple TREs at once — increases research power while maintaining privacy. However, the readiness of TRE infrastructure to support complex neuroimaging workflows has not yet been fully realised.

Our Approach

BRAID will adapt existing brain-frailty analytics pipelines into TRE-ready workflows, using advanced machine-learning on synthetic data to identify early biomarkers of brain frailty and test how well federated TREs handle complex brain data.

Public Involvement

Alongside technical work, we run workshops and engagement activities to explore public views on privacy, data use, and federated technologies — ensuring our methods reflect public values and address societal concerns.

Expected Impact

Identifying where improvements are needed will make TREvolution technologies more reliable, scalable, and user-friendly — paving the way for a national framework for federated brain health research.
