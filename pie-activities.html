Public Involvement & Engagement

Working with the public throughout BRAID to ensure federated brain research reflects societal values, addresses concerns, and meets public expectations.

About Our PIE Programme

Public Involvement and Engagement (PIE) is woven throughout the BRAID project, not added as an afterthought. Guided by the DARE UK PIE Guidelines, our programme is led by Claire Newman (SAIL Databank, Swansea University) — the UK's leading specialist in public engagement for data-intensive health research.

Our PIE activities are structured around the AREA-4P framework (Anticipate, Reflect, Engage, Act – Process, Product, Purpose, People), ensuring that public perspectives genuinely shape technical design decisions, communication strategies, and the responsible development of federated technologies.

Anticipate

Exploring ethical, social, and technical implications of federated analysis methods and the TRE technology stack before and during development.

Reflect

Critically examining data access, representation, and fairness in how brain frailty research is conducted across distributed environments.

Engage

Involving people with lived experience alongside technical and governance experts from TREvolution, SACRO, and the Federated Analytics team.

Act

Embedding continuous risk identification, communication of risks, and mitigation processes directly into project sprints and technical design decisions.

Planned Activities

PIE activities are planned across the full 12-month project timeline, engaging diverse public voices at each phase. This timeline will be updated as activities take place.

Month 1–2 · Inception

Co-Design Workshops – Scoping

Introductory workshops with members of the public and the SAIL Consumer Panel to co-design the PIE programme. Participants will help set the agenda for engagement, identify key questions about federated technologies, and establish ground rules for ongoing involvement.

Objective: Establish public advisory input into BRAID from day one.

Month 3–4 · Foundation

Public Perceptions – Data Privacy & TREs

Structured workshops exploring public understanding and attitudes towards Trusted Research Environments, data federation, and the use of brain scan data in health research. Sessions will use accessible multimedia materials developed with public contributors.

Objective: Document baseline public perceptions of TRE infrastructure and data use.

Month 4–6 · Development

Technical Design Feedback Sessions

As BRAID pipelines are developed, public contributors will be invited to review workflow transparency materials, plain-language explanations of disclosure controls, and how brain frailty data is handled across TREs. Feedback directly informs technical design decisions.

Objective: Ensure public understanding and trust in federated neuroimaging pipelines.

Month 6–8 · Evaluation

Responsible AI & Federated Analytics Workshop

Deeper engagement on responsible innovation themes: algorithmic fairness, the meaning of "brain age" models, and how AI-derived predictions about brain health should be communicated to patients and clinicians. Includes people with lived experience of neurological conditions.

Objective: Shape communication strategies and ethical framing of BRAID outputs.

Month 9–10 · Review

Findings Review & Sense-Check

Public contributors are invited to review a summary of BRAID's technical findings and provide a "sense-check" on how findings are framed and communicated. Their perspectives feed directly into the project's best-practice guidance documents.

Objective: Co-produce dissemination materials that reflect public values.

Month 11–12 · Dissemination

Public Report & Lessons Learned

Production of an anonymised public summary report synthesising views, concerns, and recommendations gathered throughout the PIE programme. This report will be shared openly and will inform future DARE-UK federated infrastructure projects.

Objective: Leave a lasting record of public perspectives for the DARE-UK community.

Our Guiding Principles

Diverse Voices

We actively seek to engage participants from diverse backgrounds, including people with lived experience of neurological conditions, cognitive decline, and frailty.

Meaningful Influence

Public input is documented and traced to specific project decisions. We report back to participants on how their contributions have shaped the work.

Transparency

All PIE activities are conducted openly. Findings — including challenges and concerns raised — are captured in anonymised reports shared with the community.

DARE UK PIE Standards

All activities align with the DARE UK PIE Guidelines, ensuring measurable outcomes and co-design at every stage.
