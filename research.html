Research

Scientific background, objectives, and related publications for the BRAID project.

Project Summary

The Brain fRAilty Integrated through Data federation (BRAID) project evaluates and enhances the DARE-UK TREvolution infrastructure to support federated neuroimaging and multimodal analytics. Our aim is to advance the long-term vision of a scalable, privacy-preserving national framework for cross-TRE research on brain health and frailty.

Brain frailty refers to the risk of poor clinical outcome — including mortality — resulting from pre-existing but not yet clinically manifest brain damage from ageing and subclinical disease. Unlike chronological ageing, brain frailty reflects cumulative structural and functional deterioration that increases vulnerability to neurodegeneration, hypoxia, or neurotoxicity.

Neuroimaging studies have identified robust indicators of brain frailty — cortical thinning, white-matter hyperintensities, microbleeds, and disrupted functional connectivity — that correlate with physical frailty, accelerated cognitive decline, poor mental health, and adverse outcomes including dementia, stroke, and treatment-related neurotoxicity.

Infrastructure Readiness

Testing compute capacity, workflow management, access procedures, interoperability, provenance tracking, and containerised pipelines within the TREvolution technology stack.

Disclosure Control

Evaluating whether analytical outputs — spatial maps, summary statistics, numerical vectors, trained models — pose any risk of re-identification before leaving the TRE.

Federated Machine Learning

Adapting multi-modal MRI processing and brain age regression models into TES (Task Execution Service) compatible workflows deployable across distributed TRE nodes.

Methodology

BRAID employs a staged, synthetic-data-driven approach using the DPUK TRE as the primary federated node, with Nottingham University Hospitals as a secondary node. All research uses synthetic data derived from real neuroimaging cohorts, ensuring privacy whilst enabling rigorous technical validation.

Synthetic Data Generation

High-fidelity synthetic datasets are generated from neuroimaging and clinical data (QMIN-MC, OBHC, MEMENTO, Whitehall II, CamCAN) using differentially private vine copula methods developed through DPUK. Datasets are evaluated for utility, quality, and privacy protection.

Neuroimaging Pipelines

Multi-modal anatomical MRI processing (T1-weighted, T2-weighted, susceptibility-weighted) using FSL and UK Biobank pipelines. Outputs include brain age regression, cortical thickness, white matter hyperintensity segmentation, and subcortical volume analysis.

FAIR Data Principles

All datasets are explicitly tagged and documented according to FAIR principles using BIDS (Brain Imaging Data Structure) format. Metadata specifications ensure reproducibility of federated neuroimaging pipelines.

TREvolution Integration

Workflows are deployed as GA4GH Task Execution Service (TES) requests — standardising submission of analysis tasks to TREs for interoperability, auditability, and security across the three-layer (Submission / Controller / Execution) TREvolution architecture.
